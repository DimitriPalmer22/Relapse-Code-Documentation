Player Data for the 
ENTIRE LEVEL.
(Player, Manager Scripts, UI, PP Volume, Music etc.) ^2ZU6pe36

Note:
When I say "level", I mean a collection of scenes / sub-scenes that make up 1 area of the game.

For example, the favelas and the apartment are both levels.

Scenes / sub-scenes are the individual scene files that make up the whole level! ^Yt69vD9D

Scriptable Object for a
Level Section
(For example, floor 3 in the apartment) ^AtIJSHFi

Persistent Data Sub-scene for 
Level Section 1
(Pickups, Vendors, Interactables, Enemies, etc.) ^4IJsm7Ke

The non-persistent sub-scene for
Level Section 1

The static & non-interactable game
objects found in Level Section 1.

(Walls, floors, ceilings) ^mv8Mv8jH

These are the things that constantly need to stay in the game's memory for as long as the player is in the level. ^OoWpk1oT

These are the things that are specific to one part of the level. We don't want them to ALWAYS be active, but we don't want them to be completely unloaded either.

For example, if I 
- put a pickup in the non-persistent sub-scene
- grabbed the pickup in-game
- unloaded this portion of the level
- and then reloaded this portion of the level

The pickup would respawn! We don't want this because it would be easy to exploit the game this way!

However, if we keep a persistent data scene JUST for our pickups and stuff and NEVER unload it, then we avoid this problem! ^yojBo6ll

These are the things that, no matter how many times we unload or reload them, they will ALWAYS stay the same. ^voyr5KP3
